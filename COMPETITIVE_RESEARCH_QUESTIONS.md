# COMPETITIVE RESEARCH — Demo Questions

**Purpose:** Question guide for demoing SimpleConsign and Aravenda as a potential customer (estate sale operator). These questions are designed to validate or challenge hypotheses in MARKET_INTEL.md and surface information we can't get from websites alone.

**Your cover (stick close to reality — it's mostly true):** You're a tech/product person — product strategist, business process analyst, senior PM background. Your friend runs a small estate sale operation in Austin, TX (solo operator, ~2 sales per month, 3–5 workers per sale, printing calculator + Square). You're helping her evaluate software options to see if anything out there is a good fit. You're doing demos with multiple companies. You have the tech knowledge to evaluate the products but you're looking at it through the lens of whether your friend — who is not technical — could actually adopt this. *(This framing is great because it lets you ask both "could my friend use this?" simplicity questions AND sharp technical/product questions without breaking character.)*

**How to use this:** Don't read these questions off a list during the demo. Internalize the themes and let questions come up naturally. The best intel comes from follow-up questions after they show you something. The starred (★) questions are the highest-priority ones to get answered.

## Before the Demo

**Research to do first:**
- Sign up for the demo/trial on their website so you see the real onboarding experience
- Note: how many steps to get started? Did they ask for a credit card? How long did it take?
- Screenshot the pricing page before the demo — sales reps sometimes offer different pricing

## Questions by Theme

### 1. Setup & Onboarding (Testing our "zero setup" hypothesis)

Our thesis is that setup burden is why operators reject these tools. These questions probe how heavy the lift actually is.

- ★ "If I signed up today, how quickly could I use this at a sale this weekend?" *(Listen for: hours of setup? inventory required? training needed?)*
- "Do I need to enter all my items into the system before a sale?" *(This is the key question — does the checkout require pre-loaded inventory?)*
- "What does my checkout worker need to know to ring up customers? How long does training take?"
- "Can I try it at one sale before committing? Like, run it alongside my calculator to compare?"
- "What happens if my worker gets confused mid-sale? Is there someone to call?"
- "Do my workers need to create accounts or log in?"

### 2. The Checkout Experience (Testing our core positioning)

We believe checkout speed is the #1 pain point and nobody is solving it directly. These questions test whether their checkout is actually fast for estate sales.

- ★ "Walk me through what happens when a customer walks up with 8 items. How does checkout work, step by step?" *(Time this mentally. Count the taps/steps. Compare to: calculator + add prices + write ticket.)*
- ★ "My items are priced with masking tape — is that a problem? Do I need to change how I price things?" *(If they require barcodes or special stickers, that's a setup burden.)*
- "How does it handle day-based discounts? Like, everything is 25% off on Day 2, 50% off on Day 3?"
- "What if a customer wants to haggle on price? Can my worker override a price on the spot?"
- "How fast is adding an item that's NOT in the system?" *(For estate sales, most items are one-of-a-kind — they won't be pre-loaded.)*
- "Can my worker do this on their phone, or do I need to buy a tablet or special hardware?"

### 3. Offline / Connectivity (Testing our "works without internet" advantage)

Our market intel says WiFi/cell signal is unreliable in residential homes. This is one of our biggest differentiators.

- ★ "We do sales in houses where cell signal is terrible. Does this work offline?" *(Listen carefully — "offline mode" often means limited functionality, not full functionality.)*
- "What happens if the internet drops mid-checkout? Do we lose the transaction?"
- "Do you need WiFi for the initial setup at the start of each sale?"
- "Has this been an issue for other estate sale clients you work with?"

### 4. The Two-Person Workflow (Testing our QR handoff insight)

Most estate sales use a checkout person and a separate payment person. This workflow is unique to estate sales and pop-up events.

- "We have one person doing checkout and a different person running Square at a payment table. How does the total get from the checkout person to the payment person?" *(If they don't have a clean answer for this, they haven't solved the estate sale workflow.)*
- "Can two people be logged in at the same time — one doing checkout and one doing payments?"
- "How does the customer's ticket get from checkout to payment? Is it a printed receipt? Digital?"

### 5. Estate Sale Fit (Testing whether they actually understand this market)

SimpleConsign and Aravenda are consignment/resale platforms that *also* market to estate sales. These questions probe how deep their estate sale understanding goes.

- ★ "How many estate sale companies use your platform?" *(Listen for specifics vs. vague "we have several." If they can't name a number, estate sales aren't a real segment for them.)*
- "What's different about how estate sale operators use your platform vs. a regular consignment store?"
- "I only do about 2 sales a month, maybe 3 in a busy month. Is your pricing model good for that kind of volume, or is it more for stores that are open every day?"
- "After a sale ends, I need to give the client (the estate) a payout report showing what sold and the commission. Can you do that?" *(This is a real need — listen for how easy or hard this is.)*
- "Do you integrate with EstateSales.NET?" *(This is the dominant listing platform — 82% of operators use it.)*

### 6. Pricing & Commitment (Testing willingness-to-pay assumptions)

Our hypothesis is that estate sale operators are price-sensitive and won't pay $99+/month for checkout alone.

- ★ "What does this cost for someone like me — 2 sales a month, 3-5 workers?" *(Get the exact number. Ask about hidden fees — per-transaction fees, payment processing fees, hardware costs.)*
- "Is there a contract or can I cancel anytime?"
- "Is there a free tier or a trial period?"
- "Do I pay during months when I'm not running sales?"
- "What do most of your estate sale customers pay per month?" *(Different from list price — they might discount.)*

### 7. Pain Points & Complaints (Learning from their failures)

These questions try to surface what their existing customers struggle with — which tells us what the market actually needs.

- "What's the most common reason people cancel or stop using you?"
- "What do estate sale operators specifically ask for that you don't have yet?"
- "If I'm being honest, my workers are not very tech-savvy — some of them are in their 60s. Has that been a problem for other clients?"
- ★ "What's the one thing about your product that you wish worked better for estate sales specifically?" *(Sales reps are sometimes surprisingly honest about this if asked directly and warmly.)*

### 8. What They're Proud Of (Learning what they think their advantage is)

- "What makes you different from PROSALE?" *(See if they know PROSALE exists. See what they think their edge is.)*
- "What's the feature your estate sale customers love the most?"
- "If I could only use one feature of your platform, what should it be?"

## During the Demo — Things to Watch For

These aren't questions to ask — they're things to observe and note.

**UX observations:**
- How many screens/taps does it take to add one item and check out one customer?
- Is the interface clean or cluttered? Could a 60-year-old figure this out?
- How big are the touch targets? Could you use this standing up, on a phone?
- How fast does it feel? Any loading spinners? Any lag?
- Is there a number pad or do you type into a text field?

**Red flags to watch for:**
- "You'll want to spend a few hours setting up your inventory before your first sale" — this is the setup burden
- "It requires a WiFi connection" — this is the connectivity gap
- "Most of our clients are retail stores / consignment shops" — estate sales aren't their focus
- "You'll need [specific hardware]" — additional cost and complexity
- Any mention of "training" that takes more than 5 minutes — their UX isn't simple enough
- Vague answers about estate sale adoption numbers — they don't have real traction in this market

**Positive signals to note:**
- Features we haven't thought of that are genuinely useful for estate sales
- UX patterns that are better than what we've designed
- Pricing models that work for event-based businesses
- Specific pain points they mention that we should address
- Any customer quotes or case studies from actual estate sale operators

## After the Demo — Debrief Checklist

Write down answers to these immediately after the demo while it's fresh:

- [ ] Could their checkout process actually replace a printing calculator at a real estate sale?
- [ ] How many taps/steps to check out one customer with 5 items?
- [ ] Does it work offline? Really?
- [ ] Does it handle the two-person (checkout + payment) workflow?
- [ ] How much setup is required before the first sale?
- [ ] What's the real price for our use case?
- [ ] How many estate sale operators are they actually serving?
- [ ] What did they show that was genuinely impressive or better than our approach?
- [ ] What confirmed our positioning (simple, fast, offline, free)?
- [ ] What challenged our assumptions — anything we need to rethink?
- [ ] Would Alissa or her operator network realistically adopt this tool? Why or why not?

## Company-Specific Notes

### SimpleConsign
- **Their pitch:** AI-powered item entry (snap photo → auto-populate). This is their headline innovation.
- **Key question to probe:** Does the AI item entry work for estate sale items (one-of-a-kind furniture, art, household goods) or is it trained on retail inventory?
- **Their weakness we expect:** Designed for permanent consignment stores with ongoing inventory. Estate sales are pop-up events with disposable inventory. Ask how they handle that.
- **Price to confirm:** $99/month for new stores (Professional Plan). Is there anything cheaper for event-based use?

### Aravenda
- **Their pitch:** Fully mobile, voice item entry, multi-channel selling. Enterprise-grade.
- **Key question to probe:** Voice entry — how does it work? Is it like ours (speak a price) or more complex (full item descriptions)?
- **Their weakness we expect:** Expensive ($289–$559/month), requires Shopify integration, designed for multi-channel resale businesses. Ask if there's a simpler/cheaper option for someone who just does estate sales.
- **Price to confirm:** Do they still require Shopify? What's the actual all-in cost?
- **Note:** Some features were in beta as of 2022. Ask what's fully live now vs. still in development.

**Pre-demo findings (from website analysis, March 2026):**

We analyzed Aravenda's website before any demo. Key findings that sharpen the questions above:

- **Carolyn Thompson is President/Board Chair,** not just a sales rep. She's doing demos personally — suggests small team. Background: AOL, Deloitte advisory, Georgetown MBA, private equity. Enterprise/corporate DNA, not scrappy estate sale experience.
- **FAQ page is 100% consignment/inventory.** Questions about consignor payouts, inventory tracking, multi-channel selling. Zero content about estate sale checkout speed, on-site workflows, or the two-person handoff.
- **Demo videos only show desktop interfaces.** No mobile checkout flow visible. Estate sales happen on phones — if their product doesn't have a strong mobile checkout story, it doesn't serve estate sales.
- **"Best Consignment Software" page** positions Aravenda against consignment competitors, not estate sale tools. PROSALE isn't mentioned. Estate sales appear to be a marketing claim, not a product focus.
- **Free trial signup was broken** during our review — page rendering issues, duplicated content. The estate sale onboarding path isn't well-maintained.
- **Leadership team skews corporate/enterprise** — AOL, Deloitte, Georgetown MBAs, private equity backgrounds. Not field-tested in the estate sale world.

**Updated questions to ask based on findings:**
- "I noticed your FAQ is mostly about consignment inventory management. How does Aravenda work specifically for estate sale checkout — the actual moment of ringing up a customer?"
- "Do you have a mobile checkout flow? Your videos seem to show the desktop version."
- "My friend doesn't have consignors — she prices items and sells them at the estate. Is there a mode that doesn't require consignor setup?"
- "How many estate sale companies are actively using Aravenda for on-site checkout specifically?" (Not just "using Aravenda" — pin it to checkout.)
